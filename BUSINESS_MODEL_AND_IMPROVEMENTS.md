## Unit Economics (Show You Understand Business)

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- Social media ads: $0.50/user
- Hospital partnerships: $0.20/user
- Word of mouth: $0/user
- **Average CAC**: $0.30/user

### Lifetime Value (LTV)
- Free user: $0 direct, $2 indirect (referrals, data)
- Premium user: $24/year Ã— 3 years = $72
- **Average LTV**: $5/user (assuming 10% premium conversion)

### LTV/CAC Ratio
- $5 / $0.30 = 16.7x
- **Healthy ratio** (>3x is good, >10x is excellent)

### Break-Even Point
- Need 2,500 premium users to cover $5,000/month costs
- At 10% conversion: need 25,000 total users
- **Achievable in 6 months**

## Questions Judges Will Ask About Money

**A**: "Three-phase approach: Free to build trust, Freemium to monetize users, B2B to scale revenue. We'll be profitable in Year 2 with just 5,000 premium users at $2/month."

### Q: "Why would anyone pay when it's free?"
**A**: "Free tier is limited to 10 chats/month. Premium adds unlimited chats, appointment booking, medical history, and family accounts. It's $2/month - less than one hospital visit."

### Q: "What if people just use ChatGPT for free?"
**A**: "ChatGPT doesn't speak Pidgin, doesn't know Nigerian hospitals, doesn't detect emergencies, and isn't medical-specific. We're not competing on price - we're competing on relevance."

**A**: "Year 1: $5k (API + hosting). Year 2: $50k (small team). Year 3: $500k (team of 10). Gemini API is cheap - $0.00025 per request. At 100k users, that's only $500/month."

**A**: "We need 2,500 premium users to cover $5k/month costs. At 10% conversion, that's 25,000 total users. We can reach that in 6 months with hospital partnerships and social media."

### Q: "What's your exit strategy?"
**A**: "Three options:
1. Acquisition by Helium Health or similar (2-3 years)
2. Series A funding to expand to other African countries (3-4 years)
