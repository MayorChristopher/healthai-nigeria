# Medical Expert Consultation Checklist

## Why You Need This
Judges asked "Did you talk with an expert?" - You need to actually do this before nationals.

## Who to Contact

### Option 1: Medical Students (Easiest)
- Your university medical school
- Friends studying medicine
- Medical student associations

### Option 2: Junior Doctors
- House officers at teaching hospitals
- Doctors doing NYSC
- Young doctors on LinkedIn/Twitter

### Option 3: Telemedicine Doctors
- Doctors at Helium Health
- Doctors at DoctorCare Nigeria
- Freelance telemedicine doctors

---

## What to Ask (30-Minute Consultation)

### Part 1: Validate Emergency Detection (10 mins)

**Question 1:** "I built an AI health assistant. Here are the symptoms I flag as emergencies. Are these correct?"

**Your List:**
- Chest pain
- Difficulty breathing
- Severe bleeding
- Unconsciousness
- Seizures
- Stroke symptoms
- Severe burns
- Head injury
- Poisoning
- Severe allergic reaction

**Ask:** "What am I missing? What should I remove?"

---

### Part 2: Validate Response Approach (10 mins)

**Question 2:** "When someone describes symptoms, I provide:
1. Possible causes
2. Severity level
3. Immediate actions
4. When to see a doctor

Is this the right approach for rural Nigerians with no immediate access to healthcare?"

**Ask:** "What should I add? What should I change?"

---

### Part 3: Safety Concerns (5 mins)

**Question 3:** "What are the biggest risks of an AI giving health advice?"

**Listen for:**
- Misdiagnosis concerns
- Medication errors
- Delayed treatment
- False reassurance

**Ask:** "How can I minimize these risks?"

---

### Part 4: Pidgin Language (5 mins)

**Question 4:** "I support Nigerian Pidgin. Are there medical terms that don't translate well?"

**Examples to discuss:**
- "My belle dey pain me" (stomach pain)
- "I no fit breathe well" (breathing difficulty)
- "Blood dey comot" (bleeding)

**Ask:** "What Pidgin phrases should I specifically handle?"

---

## What to Document

### During Consultation
Take notes on:
1. Expert's name and credentials
2. Date of consultation
3. Key recommendations
4. Changes they suggested
5. Approval/concerns they expressed

### After Consultation

Create a document:
```
EXPERT VALIDATION REPORT

Consultant: Dr. [Name], [Credentials]
Date: [Date]
Duration: [Minutes]

KEY FINDINGS:
1. Emergency detection list validated with [X] additions
2. Response approach approved with [Y] modifications
3. Safety concerns addressed through [Z] measures

RECOMMENDATIONS IMPLEMENTED:
- [List changes you made based on feedback]

EXPERT QUOTE:
"[Get a quote you can use in presentations]"

NEXT STEPS:
- [Follow-up actions]
```

## Questions to Prepare For

### From the Expert
1. "How does your AI work?"
2. "What if it gives wrong advice?"
3. "Have you tested this with real patients?"
4. "What's your liability plan?"
5. "How do you handle emergencies?"

### Your Answers
1. "Uses Google Gemini API trained on medical literature, with validation layers"
2. "We never diagnose, always include disclaimers, and flag emergencies conservatively"
3. "Not yet - this consultation is part of our validation process"
4. "Clear disclaimers, no diagnosis claims, always recommend seeing a doctor"
5. "Auto-detect, show emergency warning, recommend 3 nearest hospitals, suggest calling 112"

## Red Flags to Avoid

### Don't Say:
- "I haven't talked to any medical professionals"
- "I just used AI to build this"
- "I don't think I need medical validation"
- "It's just a demo, not real"

### Do Say:
- "I consulted with Dr. [Name] who validated our approach"
- "We implemented safety measures based on medical expert feedback"
- "This is designed to complement, not replace, medical professionals"
- "We're committed to safety and accuracy"

---

## Backup Plan (If You Can't Find Expert)

### Alternative Validation Sources
1. **WHO Guidelines** - Reference official symptom guides
2. **NHS Direct** - Study their triage system
3. **Medical Textbooks** - Basic symptom assessment
4. **Telemedicine Apps** - Analyze how they handle symptoms

### What to Say
"I validated our emergency detection against WHO guidelines and NHS Direct protocols. 
I'm actively seeking medical expert consultation and have reached out to [X] doctors."
